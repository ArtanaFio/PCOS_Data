Patient Data

Below are the clinical trial details:

  • Sample size: 200 women
  • Geographic location/distribution: DMV area
  • Age: 18 to 55 years old

Patient Demographics

Patient demographics: age, BMI, ethnicity, geographic distribution of patients, socioeconomic status

Geographic distribution of participants are categorized into location types: urban, suburban, and rural.

Socioeconomic status of participants is determined by income level: low-, mid-, high-income.

Medical History

Medical history: date of diagnosis, diagnosis criteria, severity of symptoms at the time of diagnosis, previous treatments, comorbidities, reproductive history, medication history, family history, lifestyle factors, psychosocial fatctors

The Rotterdam criteria is used to diagnose PCOS: participants show any two out of the three key characteristics of PCOS: oligo-amenorrhea, hyperandrogenism, polycystic-appearing ovarian morphology on ultrasonography.

Although there is no definitive treatment to cure PCOS itself, there are treatments to minimize symptoms; those treatments are listed under "Previous Treatments".

comorbidities: obesity, insulin resistance, type 2 diabetes, hypertension, dyslipidemia cardiovasular disease, depression, anxiety
